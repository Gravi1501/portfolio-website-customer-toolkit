1. Ethical Dilemma Response

In a challenging situation, I was pressured by a senior manager to alter team performance metrics for a quarterly report. Such actions compromise data integrity and violate ethical leadership principles. As an IT Supervisor, I chose to report the incident to HR and refused to manipulate the data, emphasizing honesty and fairness as the foundation of my role.

Leadership Justification: Ethical integrity builds trust, ensures compliance, and sustains long-term success. I referenced the ethical principle of integrity—doing what is right, even when it’s hard.

3. Personal Code of Ethics

As an IT Supervisor, I adhere to the following core ethical principles:

  • Transparency: Communicating decisions and processes clearly to all stakeholders.
  • Accountability: Taking full responsibility for my team’s performance and actions.
  • Respect: Treating everyone with fairness, dignity, and professionalism.
  • Data Privacy: Protecting sensitive information against unauthorized access or disclosure.
  • Equity: Providing equal opportunities and avoiding favoritism in team management.

4. U.S. Cultural Adaptation Reflection

Working in the U.S. IT industry involves adapting to an open, fast-paced, and collaborative environment. Compared to my home country, American teams emphasize timely feedback, flat hierarchies, and continuous improvement. I have embraced these values by adjusting my communication style, accepting constructive feedback proactively, and encouraging open discussions to enhance innovation.

My leadership strategy in the U.S. involves aligning cultural expectations with core business goals, bridging international team perspectives while fostering productivity.
